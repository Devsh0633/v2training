The Problem

You Built a Real Training Business. Your LMS Is Treating You Like an HR Department.

Most LMS platforms were built for one company training its own employees. But you are a commercial training business. You sell courses to clients. You manage dozens of organizations on one platform. You issue regulated certifications. You need an eCommerce storefront, a training scheduler, and multi-tenant portals — not a glorified HR onboarding tool.

Every hour your team spends on manual workarounds — copying course libraries, managing fake group tenants, exporting certificates as PDFs, juggling three separate tools just to schedule one class — is an hour not spent growing your training business.

💸

The Per-Learner Pricing Trap

Your platform charges per learner, so every new client you win costs you more to serve. A 5,000-learner deployment can cost $50,000+ per year on a per-seat LMS. On SimpliTrain, that number never changes.

🗂️

Multi-Client Chaos

Your LMS has no concept of client tenants. You manually copy courses, create workaround groups, and manage 30 separate logins. That is 15 to 20 hours of admin time per week — every week.

📋

Manual Certificate Chaos

Certificate issuance requires manual intervention in over 80% of non-specialized LMS setups. Your team is still emailing PDFs one by one. For a company issuing 500 certificates a month, this is a full-time job.

📅

No Training Scheduling (TMS)

Your LMS doesn't schedule instructors, venues, or virtual classrooms. That is a separate tool, or spreadsheets. 62% of training instruction is outsourced partly because internal scheduling tools are broken or missing. (Training Magazine, 2025)

🔗

Broken Integrations

52% of LMS users report integration challenges as their top pain. Syncing learner data to your CRM, HR system, and payment tools requires custom API work that breaks with every platform update. (eLearning Industry, 2025)

💰

No eCommerce or Course Catalog

Training companies that sell courses online need payment processing, enrollment gating, and a public course storefront. No standard HR LMS provides this natively. You end up on WooCommerce with a leaky Zapier bridge.

This is not a skills problem. This is a platform problem. And there is a very specific platform built for exactly what you do.

Category Definition

Two Kinds of LMS Exist. Only One Was Built for You.

Every LMS on the market falls into one of two categories. The first — HR LMS platforms — were built to train employees inside a single company. One organization. Closed ecosystem. HR owns the learner data. Training is free to employees.

The second — commercial training LMS platforms — were built to sell, deliver, and scale training programs to paying clients. Multiple organizations. Open enrollment. Revenue tied to learner volume. SimpliTrain is the second kind.
